Frogger retrieves pulses from raw binned FROG traces.

Main Panel

The first step is to load a binned FROG trace. This can be performed through the File->Open menu.

The Main Panel: No file loaded.

The main panel has some areas of note. There is of course the ubiquitous menu bar. The rest of the panel is basically divided into three columns.

In the first column, there are two plots. The upper plot, Original, is the binned FROG trace that was loaded in (It starts out with a theoretical trace) and the lower trace, Reconstructed, is the current reconstructed trace. These plots show the amplitude of the traces to accentuate the low intensity areas.

The second column displays an information area and some controls at the top. The controls will be discussed later. On the bottom, is a plot of the difference between the Original and Reconstructed plots.

The final column contain plots of the pulses involved in the retrieval and a couple of controls. A plot of the error values is also shown.
